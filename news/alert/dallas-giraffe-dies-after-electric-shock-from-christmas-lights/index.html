Young Giraffe at Dallas Zoo Dies After Eating Christmas Lights

Jasper, a young giraffe at the Dallas Zoo, tragically died after ingesting Christmas lights, leading to electrocution.

Nov 14, 10:41 AM EST 2024

Share this page
  • Share on Twitter
  • Share on Facebook
  • Share with Email
  • Print this page

Contact: media@aldf.us.org

Dalla, TX. — The Dallas Zoo is mourning the death of Jasper, a three-year-old giraffe fatally electrocuted Friday evening after ingesting a string of Christmas lights during the zoo’s Holiday in the Wild event. Despite immediate staff efforts, the injuries proved fatal.

Jasper had pulled the lights into his enclosure, mistaking them for food. Witnesses described him investigating the decorations before a loud pop and sparks ended his life.  

“This is an unimaginable loss,” said Michael Herring, the zoo’s CEO. “Jasper was a symbol of our commitment to wildlife conservation and education.”

Born in 2021 as part of an endangered Rothschild’s giraffe breeding program, Jasper was a favorite for his playful and curious personality.

The zoo has launched an investigation into how Jasper accessed the lights and temporarily closed the giraffe habitat. “We are reviewing all safety protocols to ensure this never happens again,” Herring added.

The incident has drawn criticism from animal advocates, urging zoos to prioritize animal safety during seasonal events. “Decorations must never come at the expense of wildlife safety,” said Dr. Rachel Murk.

Visitors have left flowers and notes at a memorial near the giraffe habitat. Many shared memories of meeting Jasper and the joy he brought to their visits.

Emily Jackson, a zookeeper, expressed the profound loss. “Jasper was so full of life and curiosity, always making us smile. It’s hard to imagine the habitat without him.”

The zoo plans a public memorial to honor Jasper and raise awareness of giraffe conservation. For now, the holiday lights remain dimmed, a reminder of the fragile lives they strive to protect.
